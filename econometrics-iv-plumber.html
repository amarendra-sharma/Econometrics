What you just learned

A valid instrument needs two pipes-worth of properties: a strong, testable connection to the endogenous regressor (you watched the first-stage F separate the strong pipe from the faint one), and an untestable promise of no direct leak into the outcome (you watched the leaky pipe deliver a confidently wrong 2SLS estimate, and the weak pipe deliver a wildly unstable one). Relevance you test; exclusion you argue; weakness you fear.

★ Sharma's Take

Notice the leaky pipe felt great: strong F, tight standard error, decisive estimate — and wrong. The most dangerous instrument is the one that performs beautifully on every testable dimension.
