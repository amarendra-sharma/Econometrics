What you just learned

Adding regressors can never lower in-sample R² — you proved it by watching the train meter rise on every expedition while the test meter told the real story: gains from genuine structure (the quadratic in experience, the interaction), then losses as high-order polynomials memorized the training noise. The held-out set and the ranger's cross-validation agreed, and that agreement is the discipline: specification choice is a prediction question answered with data the model has never seen.
