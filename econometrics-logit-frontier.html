What you just learned

With a binary outcome the regression function is a probability, and the unit interval is a real frontier: the LPM crosses it (you counted the impossible predictions), the logit cannot. The price of the S-curve is that one coefficient implies many marginal effects — steep where risk is near 50%, flat in the tails — which is why professionals report AMEs for audiences and local effects for individuals, and never raw index coefficients for either.
