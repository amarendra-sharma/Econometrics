Trial 1 · The Overfitting Gauntlet

Fit the curve — without memorizing the noise

You get 40 training points from a wavy economic relationship. Choose a polynomial degree and fit. Your train RMSE will keep falling as the degree rises; the test RMSE (200 fresh points) will not. Find the degree the test set loves.

Trial 2 · The Lasso Lever

One dial controls bias and variance

150 observations, 60 candidate predictors, only 4 real. The lever is the lasso penalty α. Slide it: high α strangles everything (bias), low α lets all 60 in (variance). The 5-fold cross-validation curve is your instrument — park the lever at its minimum.

Trial 3 · The Hidden-Holdout Tournament

Commit, then meet the future

A fresh problem: 140 training observations, 80 predictors, sparse truth. Choose your weapon and (if regularized) your α, then submit. Your model is scored on 100 holdout observations that are never displayed. Score 0 = no better than predicting the mean; score 100 = matches the cross-validated oracle. Beat the oracle and you go above 100.
