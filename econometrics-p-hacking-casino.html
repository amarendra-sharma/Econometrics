What you just learned

Under a true null, p-values are uniformly distributed — your histogram shows it — so 1 in 20 tests "wins" at the 5% level by design. The 5% error rate is a promise about a single pre-specified test, not a license to shop. You manufactured a discovery from nothing, watched the books expose it, and met the two honest defenses: pre-registration and multiple-testing corrections. You are now harder to fool, including by yourself.

★ Sharma's Take

Nobody p-hacks on purpose at first. It starts as "let me just check one more specification." The casino is in your own head; pre-registration is how you bar the door.
