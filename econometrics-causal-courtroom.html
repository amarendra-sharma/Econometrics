What you just learned

Identification strategies are not buttons on a menu; each one answers a specific attack. Self-selection is answered by randomization; "they were on a different path" is answered by difference-in-differences with parallel trends; "winners are just better" is answered by comparing near-identical students across a sharp cutoff; reverse causality is answered by an instrument that moves the regressor for unrelated reasons. You watched the wrong witness produce confident, biased evidence and the right witness land on the truth — the design, not the regression, is the argument.

★ Sharma's Take

In seminar, the question is never "what did you regress?" It is "what is your identification?" If you can name the threat and the witness that answers it, you are doing econometrics. Everything else is typing.
