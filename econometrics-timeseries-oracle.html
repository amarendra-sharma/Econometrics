What you just learned

Two independent random walks regularly produce a levels regression with superb fit and giant t statistics — you watched the Oracle conjure one from literal nothing. The tell is in the unit roots: ADF could not reject nonstationarity in either series, and differencing to stationarity made the "relationship" evaporate. In time series, a beautiful levels fit is an accusation, not a finding, until stationarity (or cointegration) has been established.
