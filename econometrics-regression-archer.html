What you just learned

OLS picks the line minimizing the sum of squared residuals — you verified by hand that no aim beats it. Squaring is a choice with consequences: it makes the fit a conditional mean (efficient under clean errors) and makes it sensitive to outliers, while absolute loss targets the conditional median and resists them. When you see an OLS estimate jump after adding one observation, you now know exactly which mechanism did it.

★ Sharma's Take

Every estimator is a loss function wearing a suit. Ask what it minimizes and you will know how it behaves — before the data ever arrive.
