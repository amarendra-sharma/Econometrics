What you just learned

Heteroskedasticity leaves the point estimates standing and burns the inference around them: you watched the coefficient barely move across treatments while the standard error roughly doubled under honest (robust) accounting. The thermal pattern — variance rising with the fitted values — is the most common fever in economic data, the Breusch–Pagan test formalizes the eyeball, robust SEs are the default cure, and amputating "hot" data is malpractice dressed as treatment.
